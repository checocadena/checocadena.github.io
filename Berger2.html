Hieroglyphs, utilized by the ancient Egyptians, relied on visual symbols to represent concepts, objects, and actions, and they transcended linguistic barriers. This mirrors the universal nature of contemporary symbols, such as emojis, which can convey universal emotions and reactions. It remains to be seen whether it will culminate in a language dominated by images, akin to hieroglyphs, or if a more balanced and symbiotic relationship between text and visuals will emerge as the norm in our evolving digital discourse.
